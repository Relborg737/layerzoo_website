# LayerZoo FAQ (30+ Questions)

## What is LayerZoo?
LayerZoo is a practical Bitcoin and Layer 2 education project. It provides free toolkits, inline decision trees, competitions, and resources for educators and self-learners. The goal is real adoption through teaching and Layerz Wallet usage.

## Who is the Educator Blueprint for?
Educators who want to teach Bitcoin and Layer 2 effectively. It uses a sequential decision tree to match audience knowledge, goals, and context to the best kits and teaching sequence. Primary focus for scaling impact and Layerz Wallet downloads.

## What is the recommended wallet for teaching?
Layerz Wallet. It is self-custodial, supports Bitcoin and multiple Layer 2 protocols (Lightning, Stacks, ARK, Spark, Rootstock) in one app, and is the default recommendation in all kits and the decision tree.

## How does the decision tree work?
It is fully inline on the homepage and FAQ page. Start by assessing audience Bitcoin knowledge, goals, and context. Follow one of three common paths with direct links to specific kit folders (beginner, intermediate, layer2, advanced, wallet-guide).

## What competitions does LayerZoo run?
Value-adding competitions announced in the official Telegram group. Participants must use Layerz Wallet to hold/view NFTs and submit proofs. Winners receive payouts to verified wallet addresses. Terms are in layerzoo_colaso_terms_conditions.html.

## What is self-custody and why teach it?
Self-custody means controlling your own private keys instead of trusting exchanges or custodians. It is a core Bitcoin principle. All LayerZoo materials demonstrate it using Layerz Wallet so students experience sovereignty firsthand.

## What is the difference between a coin and a token?
A coin has its own blockchain (e.g. Bitcoin, Stacks via proof-of-transfer). A token lives on another chain via smart contracts (e.g. $DUKE on Stacks). Both are covered in the jargon buster and layer2 kits.

## How does Lightning work for education?
Lightning is Bitcoin's fastest Layer 2 for instant, low-cost, private payments. Kits show how to create invoices, pay them, and use Lightning addresses in Layerz Wallet. Excellent for demonstrating scalability.

## What is ARK?
ARK is a Layer 2 protocol for Bitcoin that provides fast, cheap, private virtual UTXOs with on-chain settlement. Taught in the Layer 2 kit as a practical scaling solution.

## What is Spark?
Spark is a Layer 2 focused on scalable payments and static on-chain deposit addresses. Layerz Wallet supports it natively. Covered in advanced paths of the decision tree.

## What is Stacks?
Stacks is a Layer 2 that brings smart contracts to Bitcoin using proof-of-transfer. Popular for NFTs and Clarity contracts. Kits include Stacks-specific slide decks and wallet connections (Leather/Hiro).

## What is BitVM?
BitVM enables complex Bitcoin computation and verification without soft forks. It unlocks advanced Layer 2 capabilities and is referenced in ideological/ advanced educator paths.

## How do NFT series support learning?
Each collection (Colaso launch + upcoming series) ties to educational themes. They reward participation, create engagement for self-learners, and drive Layerz Wallet downloads.

## How do I claim a Lightning address?
Use Layerz Wallet or a compatible self-custodial app. Claim a username@layerz.me style address. The `claim_lightning_address` MCP tool and wallet UI make this easy for demonstrations.

## What is the difference between on-chain and Layer 2?
On-chain Bitcoin is the secure base layer. Layer 2 solutions (Lightning, ARK, Spark, Stacks) add speed, cost-efficiency, and features while settling to Bitcoin security. Both are taught progressively in the decision tree.

## Why is Layerz Wallet the only wallet you need?
It is self-custodial, supports Bitcoin + all major Layer 2 protocols in a single mobile app, and is the recommended tool in every LayerZoo kit for practical teaching.
