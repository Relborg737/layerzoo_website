Educator Onboarding Decision Tree v6

Practical tool to help educators assess their audience and choose effective teaching paths for Bitcoin and Layer 2 concepts.

Start by assessing your audience:

• What is their current knowledge of Bitcoin?
• What is their main goal or need?
• What is their geographic/economic context?
Common Path 1: Complete Beginners in high-inflation regions

Suggested teaching sequence:
1. Begin with why sound money matters using local currency examples
2. Introduce stable value on Bitcoin rails (USDT on Bitcoin) first
3. Demonstrate self-custody using a reliable wallet
4. Gradually introduce native Bitcoin and Layer 2 concepts
Common Path 2: Users who know other cryptocurrencies

Suggested teaching sequence:
1. Acknowledge their experience with other chains
2. Highlight Bitcoin's unique properties as the base layer
3. Show practical Layer 2 solutions (Lightning, Ark, Spark, etc.)
4. Demonstrate tools that work well across layers
Common Path 3: Ideologically driven audience

Suggested teaching sequence:
1. Focus on sovereignty, self-custody, and Bitcoin as hard money
2. Cover security best practices and avoiding third parties
3. Introduce advanced Layer 2 capabilities (BitVM, etc.)

This decision tree is designed to be a living document. Each path can be expanded with full lesson plans, slide decks, and visual aids (including NFT series for engagement).
